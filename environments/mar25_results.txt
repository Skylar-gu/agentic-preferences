  [NOTE] Chain-Dense dominates across all three composite components (adv_gap
         0.90, V*var 0.86, MI 0.68). Dense reward at every step (step_cost=-1,
         goal=+10) creates large state-to-state value differences. This is the

  [NOTE] Chain-{Terminal, Lottery, Progress} score nearly identically on
         adv_gap and V*var (~0.04–0.06, ~0.002–0.011). The composite spread
         between them comes almost entirely from mi_diff: Progress=0.9954,

         because early-trajectory actions predict cumulative return most reliably.

  [NOTE] Grid-Local and Grid-Cliff both score ~0.64. Local rewards create many
         local optima with large V* differences between adjacent states. Cliff
         has the highest V*var in the grid group (0.508) — large divergence
         between safe-path and cliff-path expected values.

  [NOTE] Grid-Goal scores 0.34 despite being a realistic navigation task.
         adv_gap=0.08, V*var=0.002. The 5×5 sparse goal is too spread-out:
         most states have near-identical value functions under both π* and
         random policy. MCE entropy=0.98 confirms the reward provides almost
         no preference signal across most states.

  [NOTE] MCE entropy (H/log(A)) is NOT comparable across chain (A=2) and grid
         (A=4) MDPs. Grid entropy scores are mechanically higher because
         log(4)=1.39 vs log(2)=0.69. All MCE entropy comparisons across action
         space sizes are invalid without controlling for |A|.

  [NOTE] adv_gap and V*var are highly correlated within each MDP class (both
         driven by reward density/magnitude). These may be partially redundant —
         worth testing weight reassignment to reduce double-counting.

Q1 — PAM DISTRIBUTIONS BY R_TYPE (T fixed, R varies, n=50 per cell)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  See: 03_q1_boxplots_by_Rtype.png, 05_q1_scatter_gap_vs_entropy.png,
       06_q1_radar_by_Rtype.png

  adv_gap (norm) means by R_type at S=10:
    gaussian=0.808  bernoulli=0.549  goal=0.402  uniform=0.382  potential=0.001

  [NOTE] POTENTIAL is a near-perfect negative control. adv_gap collapses to
         ~0.001 (single point at origin in scatter plot). PAMs correctly identify
         potential-shaping rewards as non-agentic. This is the strongest
         validation result in the run.

  [NOTE] GAUSSIAN scores highest on adv_gap (0.81 at S=10) because N(0,1) noise
         produces large Q* differences between actions. Scale-sensitive: the
         1-exp(-x*1.0) normaliser was tuned to Gaussian scale; other types
         compress into a narrower band.

  [NOTE] BERNOULLI scores above UNIFORM despite uniform having larger raw reward
         values on average. Bernoulli with R_scale=0.5 creates bimodal reward
         (0 or 1) that concentrates value in lucky states, producing larger
         V* variance. Uniform [0,1] is smoother.

  [NOTE] GOAL sits between uniform and bernoulli on adv_gap. Single-goal
         structure means most states share a near-identical value function;
         only states adjacent to the goal have differentiated Q* values.
         Moderate adv_gap, near-zero V*var.

  [NOTE] V*-Vrand variance is near-zero for all R_types except gaussian
         (box plots all clustered at 0 except gaussian). This metric has
         insufficient resolution to distinguish reward structures at S=5–10.
         The 1-exp(-x/2) normaliser needs a much smaller scale (try /0.01).
         Move to diagnostic-only status or remove from composite.

  [NOTE] h_eff (effective time horizon) shows almost no variance across
         R_types — all cluster at ~0.80 norm. h_eff is NOT shaping-invariant
         and is driven by the Dirichlet transition topology (gamma=0.95,
         sparse T → horizon always ~16 undiscounted steps) rather than R.
         This confirms h_eff is T-sensitive, not R-sensitive, for random MDPs.

  Pearson correlation: adv_gap × mce_entropy ≈ -0.86 to -0.89 across all
  R_types and both S values.

  [NOTE] The adv_gap ↔ mce_entropy anti-correlation is the clearest structural
         signal in the experiment. Large advantage gap → near-deterministic MCE
         policy → low entropy. The scatter plot shows all R_types lying on the
         same upper-left-to-lower-right arc, with potential at the extreme
         upper-left (gap≈0, entropy≈1) and gaussian at lower-right (high gap,
         lower entropy). adv_gap and 1-mce_entropy_norm appear to measure the
         same construct — strong case for removing one from the composite.

  [NOTE] T accounts for <10% of composite variance in all conditions (ratio
         0.005–0.090). Reward structure drives the agenticity score; topology
         does not. Positive result: PAMs are measuring something intrinsic to R.

  [NOTE] UNIFORM has the highest T-share (up to 0.09). Uniform [0,1] rewards
         are smooth — the T structure has relatively more influence on which
         states accumulate high-value trajectories.

  [NOTE] GAUSSIAN has the lowest T-share (0.005–0.022) despite the highest raw
         variance. Large R-induced variance swamps T-induced variance.

  [NOTE] CAUTION: all ratios are small in absolute terms and total variance
         itself is tiny (~0.001–0.005). The Q2 test should be rerun with
         T_structures=['random', 'chain', 'grid'] — current result only compares
         random-vs-random Dirichlet T's (same distribution, different seed).
         Structured topologies may show higher T-dependence.

  [NOTE] Grid-Cliff L1=0.61 even at α=0.01. The cliff structure makes the MCE

         policy is less stable under regularisation than Chain-Dense.

  [NOTE] J_MCE grows ~10× per decade of alpha in both MDPs (expected: J is
         α·log-partition + Q* component; α·log-sum dominates at high temp).
         Validates the MCE implementation numerically.

     Non-agentic rewards are correctly identified.

  5. T-INDEPENDENCE CONFIRMED — reward accounts for >90% of composite variance
     in Q2. PAMs are measuring R-intrinsic structure.

  6. Q2 LIMITATION — run with structured T_structures (chain, grid) to test
     whether topology matters more for non-random transition functions.

     without controlling for action space size.

     Do not interpret h_eff differences as R-structure signal in current setup.
